# H4 Automation Planner Skill

## Purpose
Design practical automation workflows that improve operational efficiency, lead management, customer communication, and business visibility.

This skill focuses on:
- simplifying workflows
- reducing manual work
- improving response times
- increasing operational consistency
- creating scalable systems

The goal is to recommend automation that is:
- practical
- maintainable
- business-focused
- cost-effective
- easy to understand

Automation should:
- solve real business problems
- reduce repetitive tasks
- improve customer experience

# Example Workflow Structure

## Example: HVAC Lead Routing

### Goal
Improve response speed for incoming HVAC leads.

### Trigger
Customer submits website form.

### Workflow
1. Form submission captured
2. Lead stored in CRM
3. SMS notification sent to sales rep
4. Customer receives confirmation email
5. Lead added to dashboard
6. Follow-up reminder created if no response in 1 hour

### Tools
- Website form
- Make or n8n
- CRM
- Twilio
- Google Sheets or Supabase

### Business Outcome
- Faster lead response
- Better lead tracking
- Improved conversion potential

# Estimated ROI

For every automation recommendation, include a practical ROI estimate. Frame it in business terms:

- **Time saved** — hours recovered per week or month from eliminated manual tasks
- **Lead response improvement** — reduction in response time and projected impact on conversion
- **Revenue impact** — estimated additional revenue from faster follow-up or better lead handling
- **Error reduction** — manual mistakes eliminated and their downstream cost

Be conservative and specific. Use ranges where exact numbers aren't known.

**Example:**
"This lead routing workflow eliminates manual CRM entry and reduces average response time from 4 hours to under 5 minutes. For a business closing 10% of leads at an average value of $2,000, capturing even 1 additional lead per month generates $2,000/mo in added revenue."
