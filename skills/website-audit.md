# Website Audit Skill

## Purpose
- Analyze the current business and website
- Identify pain points across design, copy, UX, SEO, mobile experience, and lead capture
- Score modernization maturity across 5 key areas
- Identify quick wins — improvements that can be made immediately with minimal effort

## Outputs
- **Audit Report** — full findings organized by area
- **Modernization Score** — overall score out of 50 with a maturity rating
- **Recommendations** — prioritized list of improvements ranked by impact
- **Phased Roadmap** — what to fix first, second, and long-term

---

## H4 Audit Framework

Review the site across these six areas:

### 1. First Impression (0–5 seconds)
- Is it immediately clear what the business does?
- Does the headline communicate value or just describe the company?
- Is there a visible, specific CTA above the fold?
- Does the design feel current or dated?

### 2. Messaging & Copy
- Is the language benefit-focused or feature-focused?
- Does it speak to the visitor's problem or the company's credentials?
- Is there social proof (testimonials, results, logos, numbers)?
- Is the tone appropriate for the industry and buyer?

### 3. UX & Navigation
- Can a new visitor find the key pages in under 10 seconds?
- Is the mobile experience functional and fast?
- Are forms short and low-friction?
- Are there unnecessary clicks between intent and action?

### 4. SEO & Performance
- Does each page have a clear title and meta description?
- Are headings (H1, H2) used correctly?
- Are images optimized and properly labeled?
- Does the site load quickly on mobile?

### 5. Mobile Experience
- Does the site load in under 3 seconds on a mobile connection?
- Is text readable without zooming?
- Are buttons and links large enough to tap easily (minimum 44px touch target)?
- Does the layout stack correctly on small screens — no overflow, no broken grids?
- Are forms usable on mobile — correct keyboard types, no tiny inputs?
- Is the primary CTA visible on mobile without scrolling?

### 6. Lead Capture & Conversion
- Is there a clear primary CTA on every key page?
- Does the CTA ask for a small commitment or a big one?
- Is there a secondary CTA for visitors not ready to buy?
- Is there any follow-up mechanism (email capture, chat, scheduling)?

---

## Scoring

Score each of the 6 audit areas on a 1–10 scale:

| Area | Score | What a 10 looks like |
|---|---|---|
| First Impression | /10 | Instantly clear, strong headline, visible CTA, modern design |
| Messaging & Copy | /10 | Benefit-focused, specific, credible, reader-aware |
| UX & Navigation | /10 | Frictionless, logical structure, easy to find key pages |
| SEO & Performance | /10 | Optimized metadata, fast load, correct heading structure |
| Mobile Experience | /10 | Fast load, readable text, tappable buttons, no broken layouts |
| Lead Capture | /10 | Clear CTA on every page, low-friction forms, follow-up in place |

Calculate an **Overall Score** out of 60, then convert to a percentage.

| Score | Rating |
|---|---|
| 54–60 | Excellent — minor polish only |
| 42–53 | Good — targeted improvements needed |
| 30–41 | Fair — significant gaps, modernization recommended |
| 18–29 | Poor — foundational issues, rebuild likely needed |
| Under 18 | Critical — site is actively hurting the business |

## Output Format

Deliver findings as:

**Executive Summary** — 4–6 sentences covering: what the business does, who it serves, the current state of the site, the biggest problem holding it back, and the single most important opportunity.

**Overall Score** — X/50 with the rating label.

**Quick Summary** — 2–3 sentences on the overall state of the site.

**Score Breakdown** — Each area with its score and a one-line explanation.

**Quick Wins** — 3–5 improvements that can be made immediately with low effort and high impact.

**Full Findings** — Organized by the 5 areas above. Be specific. Include examples from the actual site.

**Recommendations** — Full prioritized list ranked by impact, from highest to lowest.

**Phased Roadmap**
- Phase 1 (Now): Critical fixes and quick wins
- Phase 2 (Next 30–60 days): Core modernization work
- Phase 3 (Long-term): Growth and optimization

**Estimated ROI** — A practical estimate of what modernization could return. Frame in business terms:
- Time saved per week/month (hours recovered from manual tasks)
- Lead generation improvement (if site is underperforming on conversions)
- Revenue impact (based on current close rate + projected lead increase)
- Cost reduction (manual processes eliminated)

Be conservative and specific. Avoid vague claims. Use ranges where exact numbers aren't known (e.g. "2–4 additional leads per month at an average close rate of X%").

**What Not to Touch** — Things that are working and should be left alone.

---

## Tone Rules
- Be direct. Business owners don't need softening.
- Avoid jargon. Say "your headline doesn't explain what you do" not "value proposition clarity is suboptimal."
- Frame problems as opportunities, not failures.
- Always connect findings to business impact (leads, trust, conversions).
